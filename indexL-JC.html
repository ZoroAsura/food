Jeera Rice and Curry....

Fragrant basmati rice tempered with cumin meets a flavorful curry for the perfect balance of aroma and taste. Simple yet deeply satisfying, this classic duo turns any meal into pure comfort food...


Start your day with Jeera Rice and Curry!..


Ingredients....

Curry:

  • Fried onion – 1
  • Soaked cashews – 10 to 15
  • Poppy seeds – 1 teaspoon
  • Curd – 2 tbsp
  • Oil – 1 tbsp
  • Butter – 1 tbsp
  • Cumin seeds – ½ teaspoon
  • Bay leaf – 1
  • Onion – 1
  • Paste of tomatoes – 2
  • Ginger garlic paste – 1 tbsp
  • Chilli powder – 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder – 1 tsp
  • Garam masala – ½ tsp
  • Kashmiri chilli powder – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt
  • Paneer – 200 gm
  • Kasuri methi – 1 tsp

    • Jeera Rice:

  • Ghee – 2 tbsp
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Cloves – 3
  • Cardamom – 2
  • Bay leaf – 2
  • Cumin seeds – 2 tsp
  • Dry chilli – 2
  • Green chilli – 3
  • Water – 4 cups
  • Salt
  • Soaked basmati rice (30 min) – 2 cups (400 gm)
  • Coriander leaves
  • Lemon juice – 1 tsp

Instructions

  1. Step 1: For paneer masala, grind fried onion, soaked cashews, and poppy seeds into a smooth paste.
  2. Step 2: Heat oil and butter. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf. Sauté onions until slightly golden.
  3. Step 3: Add ginger garlic paste, tomato paste, and cook well. Then add the ground cashew-onion-poppy paste.
  4. Step 4: Mix in curd, chilli powder, coriander powder, garam masala, Kashmiri chilli powder, turmeric, and salt.
  5. Step 5: Add paneer cubes and kasuri methi, simmer until paneer is soft and masala thickens.
  6. Step 6: For jeera rice, heat ghee. Add cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, bay leaf, cumin seeds, dry and green chillies, and sauté.
  7. Step 7: Add soaked basmati rice and stir for a minute.
  8. Step 8: Pour in water, add salt. Cover and cook until rice is done.
  9. Step 9: Finish with coriander leaves and lemon juice. Serve hot with paneer masala curry!
