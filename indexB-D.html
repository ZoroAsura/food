Masala Dosa....

Born in Udupi, Karnataka, Masala Dosa started as a simple dosa until a clever cook added a spicy potato filling and created magic! Crispy outside, soft inside, it’s now a South Indian classic loved worldwide..


Start your day with Masala Dosa!..


Ingredients....

For Batter:

  • 3 cup sona masuri rice
  • ½ tsp methi / fenugreek seeds
  • Water, for soaking
  • 1 cup urad dal
  • 2 tbsp toor dal
  • 2 tbsp chana dal
  • 1 cup poha / avalakki, rinsed

For Aloo Bhaji:

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard
  • 1 tsp urad dal
  • 1 tsp chana dal
  • 1 dried red chilli
  • Few curry leaves
  • Pinch hing / asafoetida
  • 2 chilli, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 potato, boiled & mashed
  • 2 tbsp coriander, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Batter Preparation: Soak 3 cups sona masuri rice with ½ tsp methi for 4 hrs. Soak 1 cup urad dal, 2 tbsp toor dal, and 2 tbsp chana dal for 2 hrs. Grind dal and rice+poha separately, then mix. Ferment 8–12 hrs until doubled in volume. Add 1 tsp salt before making dosas.
  2. Aloo Bhaji: Heat 2 tbsp oil; splutter 1 tsp mustard, 1 tsp urad dal, 1 tsp chana dal, 1 dried red chilli, few curry leaves, and a pinch of hing. Add 2 chillies, 1 inch ginger, and 1 onion; sauté. Add ¼ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp salt, and 3 boiled mashed potatoes. Mix and finish with 2 tbsp coriander and 2 tbsp lemon juice.
  3. Masala Dosa: Pour a ladle of batter on a hot pan, spread thin. Add 1 tsp butter and 2 tbsp aloo bhaji in center. Cook until golden and crisp, roll, and serve with coconut chutney and sambar.
